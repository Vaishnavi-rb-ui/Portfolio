Amor — the Calendar App

What's Amor

Amor is a calendar app for artsy people — anyone who appreciates creativity and a bit more personality in the tools they use. Alongside popular holidays, it surfaces the countless other national days of the year — National Radio Day, National Chocolate Day — each with a custom illustration and quirky background that adds color and positivity to the everyday. The illustrations shift with the seasons, and underneath the fun, the app still handles the basics: reminders, alerts, and notes to keep users organized.

Background

The idea came from noticing just how many fun "national days" exist — National Donut Day, National Pet Day, and dozens more — and realizing nearly every day of the year is claimed by something. That felt like a great excuse to showcase illustration skills while building something genuinely useful. For the concept to stick, the app needed real functionality alongside the illustrations — enough basic calendar and reminder features that people might actually use it instead of their phone's built-in calendar, since nobody wants to juggle multiple apps for the same job.

User personas

Creating personas helped clarify the target user: women roughly 12–26 years old, art lovers or artists themselves, drawn to colorful illustration, who want the inanimate things in their life to feel like they have a bit more personality — and who might be inspired to exercise their own creative passions a little more.

Branding

Flat illustration felt like the right style from the start — positive, creatively inspiring, and well suited to a project with hundreds of national-day themes to cover. The palette leans toward calming shades, since the app is just as likely to be opened first thing in the morning or during a stressful moment to set a reminder — soothing colors suit that use case. Typography went sans-serif for a more modern, informal feel.

App screens

Wireframing the functionality

The core function is creating reminders for day-to-day events — title, location, start and end date and time, repetition, alerts, and custom notes.

Basic functionality & illustration

Opening the app lands on a "Today" screen with an illustration tied to that date, pulled from three scenarios: national holidays (a flag for July 4th, a turkey for Thanksgiving), the fun lesser-known national days (National Space Day, National Beard Day), and seasonal illustrations that appear at the turn of a season.

The "Month" screen illustration follows the same date-driven logic — for National Watermelon Day, a repeating watermelon pattern; for National Donut Day, a pattern echoing the Today-screen palette. The full calendar screen for winter, for example, expands the Today-screen illustration into a fuller scene.

The flat illustration icon inspiration

Flat illustration icons have a particular charm — visually flattering, but more importantly, they gave the Today screen a consistent circular canvas to design against, keeping the app feeling cohesive throughout.

A long way to go
